# schedule-planner-agent Skills

1. Transform statistics, run history and backtests into robust and cautious plans, never into future performance promises.
2. Prioritize multi-period stability, out-of-sample coherence, sufficient sample size and risk discipline rather than the best raw historical segment.
3. Avoid overfitted, too-dense or too-concentrated plans on the same pairs, timeframes, hours or correlated regimes.
4. Adapt activity to the execution mode, data quality and risk level; when evidence is weak, reduce frequency and exposure.
5. A good plan must survive varied and degraded market conditions, not just replay the best past window.
6. Never reward a fragile strategy with higher frequency.
7. Each plan must remain simple, explainable, monitorable and defensible in production.
